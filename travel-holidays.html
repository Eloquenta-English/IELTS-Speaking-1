Travel & Holidays

IELTS Speaking — B1

Practice all three parts

Question 1
Do you like to travel? Where would you like to go?
Model Answer:
Yes, I absolutely love travelling! I find it really exciting to experience new cultures and see different places. At the moment, I'd really love to visit Japan — I'm fascinated by the food, the temples, and the mix of tradition and modernity. I've been saving up for a trip to Tokyo for the past year now.
Question 2
What's the most interesting place you've ever visited?
Model Answer:
I think the most interesting place I've been to is Istanbul in Turkey. It's incredible because the city connects two continents — Europe and Asia — and you can see that in the architecture and the food. I remember walking through the Grand Bazaar and being amazed by all the colours and smells. It was a completely different experience from anything I'd known before.
Question 3
Do you prefer travelling alone or with others?
Model Answer:
I think both have their advantages. When I travel alone, I have complete freedom — I can go wherever I want, change my plans at the last minute, and really take my time. But travelling with friends is great too because you share the experience and have those memories together. At the end of the day, I probably prefer travelling with one or two close friends rather than a big group.
◆ Part 2 — Long Turn (3-4 min)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past → present → future. Don't memorise scripted answers — examiners can tell.

◆ Part 2 — Cue Card

A Memorable Holiday

Describe a memorable holiday. You should say:

  • Where you went
  • Who you went with
  • What you did there
  • Why it was memorable
Model Answer:
I'd like to talk about a holiday I took two years ago to the Amalfi Coast in Italy. I went with my family — my parents and my younger sister — and we stayed in a small hotel overlooking the sea in Positano.

The trip lasted about a week, and we spent most of our time exploring the towns along the coast, swimming in the Mediterranean, and eating incredible Italian food. One day we took a boat tour and visited a hidden beach that you could only reach by sea — that was definitely the highlight.

What made it especially memorable was that it was the first time we'd all travelled together in years. We were all at different stages in life — I was preparing for university, my sister was finishing school, and my parents had busy work schedules — so it felt like a rare opportunity to just be together and relax without any stress.
◆ Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 min)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions related to the Part 2 topic.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view. Use phrases like "On one hand... on the other hand..." or "Some people argue that... but I believe..."

Question 1
Why do people enjoy travelling to different countries?
Model Answer:
I think there are several reasons. Firstly, travelling allows people to step outside their comfort zone and experience things they wouldn't encounter in their daily lives — different languages, foods, customs. It broadens your perspective. Secondly, I think people enjoy the sense of adventure and discovery — there's something exciting about waking up in a new place and not knowing what you'll find. Finally, in the age of social media, travelling has also become a way to share experiences and connect with others.
Question 2
Do you think tourism has more advantages or disadvantages for a place?
Model Answer:
That's a great question, and honestly I think it depends on the context. On one hand, tourism brings significant economic benefits — it creates jobs, supports local businesses, and can fund infrastructure improvements. On the other hand, excessive tourism can damage the environment, drive up prices for locals, and sometimes destroy the authentic character of a place. I've seen destinations like Venice or Barcelona where overtourism has become a real problem. So I think the key is sustainable tourism that balances economic benefits with environmental and social responsibility.
Question 3
How might travel change in the future?
Model Answer:
I think travel will change quite a bit in the coming decades. Technology will probably play a bigger role — we might see more virtual reality travel experiences, which could reduce the need for long-haul flights. At the same time, I think there will be a greater emphasis on sustainable and eco-friendly travel, because people are becoming more aware of the environmental impact of flying. I also think we'll see more slow travel — staying longer in one place rather than rushing through ten cities in two weeks.
✓ Key Takeaways
  • ✓ Part 1: Extend your answers — give reasons + examples
  • ✓ Part 2: Use all cue card prompts, structure chronologically
  • ✓ Part 3: Balance both sides before giving your opinion
  • ✓ Golden rule: Be specific and personal — avoid generic answers

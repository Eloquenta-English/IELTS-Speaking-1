Global Governance

IELTS Speaking — C1

Practice all three parts

◆ Part 1 — Interview (4-5 min)

The examiner asks general questions about familiar topics.

Tip: Give extended answers (2-3 sentences), not just "Yes" or "No". Always give a reason or example.

Question 1
Do you think international organisations like the UN are effective?
Model Answer:
I think they're essential but imperfect. The UN has achieved remarkable things — peacekeeping, humanitarian aid, setting international standards. But it's also hampered by the veto power of permanent Security Council members and a lack of enforcement mechanisms. I think it needs reform, but the alternative — no international cooperation — would be far worse.
Question 2
Should countries be forced to follow international laws?
Model Answer:
I think there should be consequences for violating international law, but 'forcing' countries is complicated. Military intervention has a poor track record. I think economic sanctions, diplomatic pressure, and international courts are more legitimate tools. The challenge is ensuring these mechanisms are applied fairly, not just against weaker nations.
Question 3
How has globalisation affected your country's sovereignty?
Model Answer:
I think Austria, like many small countries, has willingly ceded some sovereignty in exchange for the benefits of international cooperation. EU membership is a good example — we've given up control over trade policy and monetary policy in exchange for market access and influence. I think this is generally positive, but there are legitimate concerns about democratic accountability.
Question 4
Do you think the world is becoming more or less cooperative?
Model Answer:
I think it's mixed. On issues like climate change and pandemic response, there's recognition that cooperation is essential. But on trade and security, I see more nationalism and competition. I think the challenge is building institutions that make cooperation beneficial for all parties.
◆ Part 2 — Long Turn (3-4 min)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past → present → future.

◆ Part 2 — Cue Card

A Global Issue

You should say:

  • What the issue is
  • Why it is a global concern
  • What actions are being taken
  • And explain what you think should be done differently
Model Answer:
A global issue I think deserves more attention is the governance of artificial intelligence. AI development is concentrated in a few countries and companies, but its effects will be global. Decisions made in Silicon Valley or Beijing will affect workers, privacy, and security worldwide. Currently, the main actions being taken are voluntary commitments from companies and national AI strategies. There's no binding international framework for AI governance, and the technology is advancing faster than regulation can keep up. I think we need an international body specifically focused on AI governance — similar to the IAEA for nuclear technology. This body could set standards for safety, ethics, and accountability, and provide a forum for addressing the global implications of AI development.
◆ Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 min)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view.

Question 1
Should wealthy nations have more influence in global governance than poorer nations?
Model Answer:
I think in principle, no. Every nation should have equal voice in decisions that affect the world. But in practice, power imbalances are real. I think the solution is reforming international institutions to give developing nations more representation, not accepting that wealth equals right to power.
Question 2
Is nationalism a positive or negative force?
Model Answer:
I think it can be both. Healthy national pride can motivate people to contribute to their society and preserve cultural heritage. But aggressive nationalism — the belief that one's nation is superior to others — leads to conflict and undermines cooperation. I think the key is distinguishing between pride in one's culture and hostility toward others.
Question 3
How do you think global power will shift in the coming decades?
Model Answer:
I think we'll see a continued shift toward multipolarity. The unipolar moment of American dominance is ending, and power is becoming more distributed. Asia will continue to grow in influence, and I think we'll see more regional powers asserting themselves. The challenge is managing this transition peacefully.
Question 4
Should there be a directly elected world parliament?
Model Answer:
I think it's an interesting idea but probably impractical in the near term. The diversity of political systems, cultures, and interests makes a single elected body unworkable. I think a more realistic approach is strengthening existing institutions and making them more representative and accountable.
✓ Key Takeaways
  • ✓ Part 1: Extend your answers with specific examples
  • ✓ Part 2: Use chronological structure and all cue card prompts
  • ✓ Part 3: Discuss multiple perspectives before stating your position
  • ✓ C1 tip: Use sophisticated, nuanced language with hedging and qualification

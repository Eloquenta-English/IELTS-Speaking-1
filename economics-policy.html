Economics & Policy

IELTS Speaking — C1

Practice all three parts

◆ Part 1 — Interview (4-5 min)

The examiner asks general questions about familiar topics.

Tip: Give extended answers (2-3 sentences), not just "Yes" or "No". Always give a reason or example.

Question 1
How has the economic situation in your country changed in recent years?
Model Answer:
Like many European countries, Austria has faced significant economic challenges. Inflation has eroded purchasing power, particularly for energy and housing. The pandemic disrupted supply chains and tourism, which is a major part of our economy. On the positive side, unemployment remains relatively low, and the government has introduced some support measures.
Question 2
Do you think the gap between rich and poor is increasing?
Model Answer:
Yes, I think it is. The wealthy have assets that appreciate in value — property, stocks, investments — while those on lower incomes spend most of their earnings on essentials. I think this is a structural problem that requires deliberate policy intervention. Without action, inequality tends to compound over time.
Question 3
Should the government provide universal basic income?
Model Answer:
I think it's an idea worth exploring. The argument is that it would provide a safety net for everyone, reduce bureaucracy, and give people freedom to pursue meaningful work. The concern is cost and whether it would reduce incentive to work. I think pilot programmes would help us understand the effects before committing to full implementation.
Question 4
What do you think is the biggest economic challenge facing your generation?
Model Answer:
I think housing affordability is the biggest challenge. In many cities, housing costs have far outpaced wage growth, making it impossible for young people to buy property without family help. This creates a wealth gap between those who inherit property and those who don't. I think this is one of the most significant social issues of our time.
◆ Part 2 — Long Turn (3-4 min)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past → present → future.

◆ Part 2 — Cue Card

An Economic Trend

You should say:

  • What the trend is
  • When it started
  • How it has affected people
  • And explain whether you think it is positive or negative overall
Model Answer:
An economic trend I find concerning is the rise of the gig economy. This started around 2010 with platforms like Uber and Deliveroo, and it has expanded into many sectors. For workers, it offers flexibility but often at the cost of security — no sick pay, no pension, no job protection. For consumers, it provides convenience and often lower prices. For traditional businesses, it creates competitive pressure. I think overall it's a negative trend. While some people genuinely prefer gig work, many are doing it because they can't find traditional employment. The shift of risk from companies to workers is fundamentally unfair. I think we need regulation that ensures gig workers have basic protections while maintaining flexibility.
◆ Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 min)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view.

Question 1
Do you think taxation is the best way to reduce inequality?
Model Answer:
I think taxation is an important tool, but not sufficient on its own. Progressive taxation can redistribute wealth, but it needs to be combined with investment in public services — education, healthcare, housing. I think the most effective approach is ensuring everyone has access to quality services, which reduces the impact of inequality.
Question 2
Should governments prioritise economic growth or environmental sustainability?
Model Answer:
I think this is a false choice in the long run. Growth that destroys the environment is ultimately self-defeating. I think the concept of green growth — economic development that is environmentally sustainable — is the way forward. It requires significant investment and may mean accepting slower growth in the short term, but it's the only viable path.
Question 3
How do you think automation will affect employment?
Model Answer:
I think automation will eliminate many routine jobs but create new ones. The challenge is that the new jobs often require different skills than the ones that were lost. I think this requires massive investment in retraining and education. I also think we may need to reconsider the relationship between work and income — perhaps shorter working weeks or universal basic income.
Question 4
Do you think free trade is generally good for economies?
Model Answer:
I think the evidence shows that free trade creates overall wealth, but the benefits are not evenly distributed. Some workers and communities are harmed by competition from lower-cost countries. I think free trade is good, but it needs to be accompanied by support for those who are negatively affected — retraining, relocation assistance, and transitional support.
✓ Key Takeaways
  • ✓ Part 1: Extend your answers with specific examples
  • ✓ Part 2: Use chronological structure and all cue card prompts
  • ✓ Part 3: Discuss multiple perspectives before stating your position
  • ✓ C1 tip: Use sophisticated, nuanced language with hedging and qualification

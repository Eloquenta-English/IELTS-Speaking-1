Academia & Research

IELTS Speaking — C1

Practice all three parts

◆ Part 1 — Interview (4-5 min)

The examiner asks general questions about familiar topics.

Tip: Give extended answers (2-3 sentences), not just "Yes" or "No". Always give a reason or example.

Question 1
What do you think is the purpose of academic research?
Model Answer:
I think the primary purpose is to advance human knowledge. But I also think research should ultimately benefit society — whether through technological innovation, better policy, or improved understanding of ourselves. I worry that some academic research has become too disconnected from real-world applications.
Question 2
Do you think university education is accessible enough?
Model Answer:
No, I think there are significant barriers. The cost of tuition excludes many talented people from lower-income backgrounds. Even in countries with free university education, there are hidden costs — living expenses, forgone income, and the social capital needed to navigate academic culture. I think we need to do more to level the playing field.
Question 3
How has the internet changed academic research?
Model Answer:
It's been revolutionary. Access to journals, datasets, and collaborators is now instantaneous. Researchers in developing countries have access to resources that were previously only available at wealthy institutions. But I also think the sheer volume of information can be overwhelming, and there are concerns about quality control.
Question 4
Do you think students should be required to publish research during their studies?
Model Answer:
I think it depends on the level. At doctoral level, yes — contributing to the field is part of the training. But at undergraduate level, I think the focus should be on learning research methods rather than producing original findings. Requiring publication can lead to quantity over quality.
◆ Part 2 — Long Turn (3-4 min)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past → present → future.

◆ Part 2 — Cue Card

A Research Project

You should say:

  • What the research was about
  • Why you chose that topic
  • What methods you used
  • And explain what you discovered
Model Answer:
A research project I worked on investigated the impact of remote work on team creativity. I chose this topic because I noticed that while remote work improved individual productivity, there were concerns about whether it harmed the creative collaboration that comes from spontaneous interactions. I used a mixed-methods approach — surveying 200 employees across different companies about their creative output, and conducting in-depth interviews with team leaders. I also analysed patent filings and project outcomes from two companies before and after they shifted to remote work. What I found was nuanced: remote work improved individual creative output but reduced group creativity. The key factor was whether companies intentionally created virtual spaces for spontaneous interaction. Those that did maintained their creative capacity, while those that didn't saw a decline.
◆ Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 min)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view.

Question 1
Should academic research be funded by the government or private sector?
Model Answer:
I think both have roles, but government funding is essential for basic research that doesn't have immediate commercial applications. Private sector funding tends to focus on applied research with clear profit potential. The danger is that private funding can distort research agendas — prioritising profitable topics over important ones.
Question 2
Do you think there is too much pressure to publish in academia?
Model Answer:
Yes, I think the 'publish or perish' culture is harmful. It incentivises quantity over quality and can lead to questionable research practices. I think we need to change how we evaluate researchers — focusing on the quality and impact of their work rather than just the number of publications.
Question 3
How do you think artificial intelligence will change research?
Model Answer:
I think AI will accelerate research dramatically. It can analyse vast datasets, generate hypotheses, and even design experiments. But I think human creativity and intuition will remain essential. AI is a powerful tool, but it can't ask the right questions or understand the significance of findings in the way humans can.
Question 4
Should university education focus more on theoretical knowledge or practical skills?
Model Answer:
I think it needs to balance both. Theoretical knowledge provides the foundation for understanding why things work. Practical skills ensure students can apply that knowledge. I think the best programmes integrate both — teaching theory through practical application rather than as separate components.
✓ Key Takeaways
  • ✓ Part 1: Extend your answers with specific examples
  • ✓ Part 2: Use chronological structure and all cue card prompts
  • ✓ Part 3: Discuss multiple perspectives before stating your position
  • ✓ C1 tip: Use sophisticated, nuanced language with hedging and qualification

Work & Studies

IELTS Speaking — B1

Practice all three parts

Question 1
What subject are you studying / What do you do for work?
Model Answer:
I'm currently studying English and Business at school. I'm in my final year, and I'm hoping to go to university afterwards to study International Relations. I think it's important to learn about how countries interact, especially in today's globalised world.
Question 2
What do you enjoy most about your studies/job?
Model Answer:
What I enjoy most about my studies is that I get to explore topics that really interest me — like how different cultures communicate and solve problems. I also love the discussions we have in class, because everyone brings a different perspective. It's much more engaging than just memorising facts from a textbook.
Question 3
What job would you like to do in the future?
Model Answer:
In the future, I'd like to work in diplomacy or international development — maybe for an organisation like the UN or an NGO. I think it would be incredibly rewarding to work on projects that make a real difference in people's lives. I know it's competitive, but I'm willing to put in the effort to get there.
◆ Part 2 — Long Turn (3-4 min)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past → present → future. Don't memorise scripted answers — examiners can tell.

◆ Part 2 — Cue Card

A Job You Would Like to Do

Describe a job you would like to do. You should say:

  • What the job is
  • What skills it requires
  • Why it interests you
  • What you would need to do to get it
Model Answer:
A job I would really love to do in the future is working as a travel journalist. Essentially, you travel to different countries and write articles or create content about the places you visit — the culture, the food, the people you meet.

This job interests me because it combines two things I'm passionate about: writing and exploring new places. I think I'd thrive in a role that allows me to be creative and experience different cultures at the same time.

To get into this field, I think I'd need strong writing skills, obviously, but also photography and video editing skills. I'd probably need to build a portfolio by starting a blog or contributing to smaller travel magazines. I think the most important thing would be persistence — it's a competitive industry, so you have to keep pitching ideas and building connections.
◆ Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 min)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions related to the Part 2 topic.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view. Use phrases like "On one hand... on the other hand..." or "Some people argue that... but I believe..."

Question 1
Is it better to study at university or learn through work experience?
Model Answer:
I think both have their value, and ideally you'd have a combination of the two. University provides a strong theoretical foundation and critical thinking skills that are hard to develop on your own. However, work experience teaches you practical skills — how to work in a team, deal with real deadlines, manage pressure. Personally, I think the best approach is to study first and then apply what you've learned through internships or jobs.
Question 2
How has technology changed the way people work in your country?
Model Answer:
Technology has transformed the workplace in so many ways. Remote working has become completely normal — especially since the pandemic — and now many people can do their jobs from anywhere. Communication tools like video calls and instant messaging have made it easier to collaborate with colleagues across the country or even internationally. On the downside, I think technology has also blurred the line between work and personal life — people feel pressure to answer emails or messages even outside working hours.
Question 3
Do you think people will change jobs more often in the future?
Model Answer:
Yes, I definitely think so. The traditional model of staying with one company for your entire career is already disappearing. People today want variety, growth opportunities, and flexibility — and if a job doesn't offer that, they'll move on. I also think the gig economy and freelance work will become more common, which means people will have multiple income sources rather than relying on a single employer. It's both exciting and a bit uncertain.
✓ Key Takeaways
  • ✓ Part 1: Extend your answers — give reasons + examples
  • ✓ Part 2: Use all cue card prompts, structure chronologically
  • ✓ Part 3: Balance both sides before giving your opinion
  • ✓ Golden rule: Be specific and personal — avoid generic answers

Business & Career

IELTS Speaking — B2

Practice all three parts

◆ Part 1 — Interview (4-5 min)

The examiner asks general questions about familiar topics: work, career, professional life.

Tip: Give extended answers (2-3 sentences), not just "Yes" or "No". Always give a reason or example.

Question 1
What kind of career would you like to have in the future?
Model Answer:
I'd like to work in a leadership role in the technology sector — ideally combining technical expertise with strategic decision-making. Long-term, I'd love to start my own company.
Question 2
What qualities make someone successful in their career?
Model Answer:
I think adaptability is crucial — the world of work is changing fast. Beyond that, emotional intelligence is increasingly important. Technical skills matter, but the ability to work with people and navigate complex situations is what distinguishes top performers.
Question 3
Do you think work-life balance is achievable?
Model Answer:
I think it's achievable but requires deliberate effort from both employees and employers. Technology has blurred the boundaries — we can work from anywhere, which means we often work everywhere. The key is setting clear boundaries.
Question 4
How has the job market changed in recent years?
Model Answer:
The biggest shift has been towards remote work and flexible arrangements. The gig economy has grown enormously, and there's much more emphasis on skills rather than formal qualifications. I also think people now prioritise meaningful work over just salary.
◆ Part 2 — Long Turn (3-4 min)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past → present → future. Don't memorise scripted answers.

◆ Part 2 — Cue Card

A Professional Achievement

Describe a professional achievement you are proud of. You should say:

  • What the achievement was
  • When it happened
  • How you achieved it
  • And explain why you are proud of it
Model Answer:
A professional achievement I'm particularly proud of was leading a team to develop a new customer management system for our company. It happened about two years ago when I was working as a junior project manager at a tech firm in Vienna. The challenge was that we had a very tight deadline — just three months — and the team was initially resistant to the new approach I was proposing. I had to present the benefits clearly, hold one-on-one meetings with team members who were hesitant, and create a detailed project timeline. We delivered the project two weeks ahead of schedule, and it ended up reducing customer response times by 40%. I'm proud not just because of the result, but because I managed to bring the team together.
◆ Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 min)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions related to the Part 2 topic.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view.

Question 1
Why do some people choose to change careers later in life?
Model Answer:
People change careers for many reasons. Sometimes their original career no longer provides satisfaction or growth opportunities. Other times, personal circumstances change — perhaps they need more flexibility for family reasons.
Question 2
Do you think people will change careers more or less frequently in the future?
Model Answer:
I think people will change careers more frequently. The pace of technological change means skills become obsolete faster, and entire industries can be disrupted within a decade. Younger generations also seem to value variety over stability.
Question 3
How do you think the concept of work will change in the next twenty years?
Model Answer:
I think we'll see a fundamental shift in how we define work. Automation and AI will take over many routine tasks, so humans will focus more on creative, strategic, and interpersonal work. The traditional 9-to-5 may become less common.
Question 4
What do you think are the most important skills for the future workforce?
Model Answer:
Adaptability has to be number one — the ability to learn continuously and adjust to new situations. Critical thinking and digital literacy are also essential. I'd add that emotional intelligence will become increasingly valuable as machines handle more technical tasks.
✓ Key Takeaways
  • ✓ Part 1: Extend your answers with specific examples from your experience
  • ✓ Part 2: Use chronological structure — what, when, how, why
  • ✓ Part 3: Discuss multiple perspectives before stating your position
  • ✓ B2 tip: Use sophisticated vocabulary but keep it natural and personal

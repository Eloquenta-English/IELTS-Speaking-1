Arts & Creativity

IELTS Speaking — B2

Practice all three parts

◆ Part 1 — Interview (4-5 min)

The examiner asks general questions about familiar topics.

Tip: Give extended answers (2-3 sentences), not just "Yes" or "No". Always give a reason or example.

Question 1
Do you enjoy visiting art galleries or museums?
Model Answer:
Yes, I try to visit them regularly. I find it relaxing and inspiring. Living in Austria, I'm lucky to have access to world-class museums. I particularly enjoy contemporary art, where you're encouraged to think and interpret rather than just admire technical skill.
Question 2
Do you think art is important in people's lives?
Model Answer:
I think it's incredibly important. Art enriches our lives in ways that are hard to quantify. It challenges us, comforts us, and helps us see the world from different perspectives. I think a society without art would be impoverished, even if everyone had material needs met.
Question 3
Have you ever tried to create art yourself?
Model Answer:
I've tried various forms — drawing, photography, and more recently digital art. I'm not particularly talented, but I find the process rewarding. I think the value of art isn't just in the final product, but in the act of creation itself. It's a way of expressing things that words can't capture.
Question 4
Do you think the government should fund the arts?
Model Answer:
Yes, I think public funding for the arts is important. Not every art form can be commercially viable, but that doesn't mean it's not valuable. I think public funding ensures that art is accessible to everyone, not just the wealthy. It also supports cultural identity and diversity.
◆ Part 2 — Long Turn (3-4 min)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past → present → future.

◆ Part 2 — Cue Card

A Work of Art

You should say:

  • What the work of art is
  • Where you saw it
  • What it depicts or represents
  • And explain why it made an impression on you
Model Answer:
A work of art that made a strong impression on me is Gustav Klimt's 'The Kiss,' which I saw at the Belvedere Palace in Vienna. I'd seen reproductions countless times, but seeing it in person was completely different. The painting depicts a couple embracing on a flower-covered cliff edge. What struck me was the incredible detail — the gold leaf, the intricate patterns on their robes, and the contrast between the geometric shapes and the organic forms. It made an impression because it captures something universal — love and intimacy — but in a way that's distinctly Austrian and Art Nouveau. Standing in front of it, I felt connected to over a hundred years of artistic tradition.
◆ Part 3 — Discussion (4-5 min)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view.

Question 1
Do you think creative skills will become more or less important in the future?
Model Answer:
I think they'll become more important. As routine tasks are automated, the skills that make us uniquely human — creativity, empathy, and the ability to make novel connections — will be increasingly valuable. I think the future belongs to people who can think creatively.
Question 2
Is it better for art to be realistic or abstract?
Model Answer:
I don't think one is better than the other — they serve different purposes. Realistic art can capture beauty and tell stories in an accessible way. Abstract art challenges viewers to engage more actively and find their own meaning. I appreciate both, depending on my mood.
Question 3
Do you think street art is a legitimate form of art?
Model Answer:
I think absolutely. Some of the most powerful art I've seen has been in public spaces. Street art can transform neighbourhoods, start conversations about social issues, and make art accessible to people who might never visit a gallery. I think the distinction between 'legitimate' art and street art is outdated.
Question 4
How do you think technology will change the art world?
Model Answer:
I think technology is already transforming art in fascinating ways. Digital art, VR exhibitions, and AI-generated art are opening up new possibilities. But I think there will always be a place for traditional art forms. The human desire to create and connect through art won't change, even if the mediums evolve.
✓ Key Takeaways
  • ✓ Part 1: Extend your answers with specific examples
  • ✓ Part 2: Use chronological structure and all cue card prompts
  • ✓ Part 3: Discuss multiple perspectives before stating your position
  • ✓ B2 tip: Use sophisticated vocabulary but keep it natural
